Hotel Scores

Hotel scores are crucial metrics that measure our performance across all Embassy Suites nationwide.

  • These scores reflect guest satisfaction, problem identification, resolution, overall cleanliness, service quality, and Hilton Honors recognition.
  • They directly impact our reputation and participation in Hilton's recognition programs.
  • Your service plays a critical role in influencing these scores. Ensuring excellent guest experiences is key to maintaining high scores and success.

Money Handling

  • The Bank must be counted in and out for each shift by an agent. While counting, the agent must have a witness.
  • One agent will "own" the bank per shift. This agent will be responsible for the bank key and count.
  • If cash is taken, the amount must be dropped at the end of the shift with a witness.

Uniforms

  • Agents are expected to be in full uniform by the time their shift starts.
  • If agents have not yet received their uniforms, dark business casual clothes are required and must be approved by the MOD (Manager on Duty).
  • Agents may be asked to leave the hotel and return in appropriate attire if the uniform requirement is not met.

Front Desk Conduct

Front desk agents should maintain a professional demeanor at all times when guest-facing. This includes:

  • No personal phones
  • No consuming food and drinks while guest-facing
  • Maintain good posture
  • Greet guests within 5-10 seconds of entering the lobby, ensuring a warm and welcoming atmosphere

Hotel Phone Policy

  • When answering the phone at the front desk, agents should start with a pleasant greeting, stating the full hotel name: "Thank you for calling the Embassy Suites at Centennial Olympic Park. This is [Your name]. How may I assist you?"
  • Speech should be pleasant and clear, ensuring the caller can sense their smile and friendliness through the phone.
  • International calls can not be made using the front desk phones
  • No personal calls are permitted

Computer Applications Needed for Front Desk Staff

  • PEP - Used to manage hotel operations, including reservations, check-in/check-out, guest profiles, billing, and housekeeping.
  • Kipsu - Messaging platform to communicate with guests via SMS
  • Quore - Used to communicate maintenance and housekeeping requests to the proper department.
  • Outlook - The platform all staff use for email.
  • *All of these applications should be logged into for your entire shift

Time & Attendance

  • Download My Hotel Team to stay updated with schedules
  • Put in request-offs in PayCom at LEAST 2 weeks in advance to be considered. Anything less won't be considered, and you must arrange your own coverage for your shift.
  • Show up on time, do not leave early. A 30-minute lunch break is required on all shifts only
  • For callouts, communicate at least 2 hours prior with MOD. The best way to communicate would be by calling the hotel directly. Anything less is an improper callout.
  • Tardiness must be communicated to the MOD
  • No guaranteed or set schedules, preferences will be kept in mind.
