Paid Time Off (PTO)

The Company's PTO program is offered to all eligible full-time team members. PTO may be used for a variety of reasons including vacation, sick, personal, holidays, or birthdays. The amount of PTO time earned is based on length of service.

Full-Time Hourly Team Members

PTO days are based upon the number of hours worked excluding overtime. PTO begins to accrue on the date of hire and may be taken after 90 days of continuous employment.

Years of Service Days Accrued Per Year
0 days to 1 year 10 days (3.07 hours per pay period)
2 to 5 years 15 days (4.61 hours per pay period)
6 to 10 years 20 days (6.15 hours per pay period)
10+ years 25 days (7.69 hours per pay period)

Full-Time Exempt (Salaried) Team Members

Salaried team members accrue PTO based upon length of service. PTO begins to accrue on the date of hire and may be taken after 90 days of continuous employment.

Years of Service Maximum Hours Per Year Maximum Days Per Year
0 days to 1 year 80 hours (0.03846 per hour worked) 10 days
2 to 5 years 120 hours (0.05769 per hour worked) 15 days
6 to 10 years 160 hours (0.07692 per hour worked) 20 days
10+ years 200 hours (0.09615 per hour worked) 25 days

PTO Request Requirements:

  • PTO must be requested a minimum of 14 days in advance and must be approved by your supervisor/manager
  • An exception may be granted at the discretion of your supervisor for a shorter duration based on business demands
  • PTO does not accrue during unpaid leave of absence or days off without pay
"Use It or Lose It" Policy: Any accrued and unused PTO may be carried over up to 90 days past your anniversary date. After that time, any unused days are forfeited.

PTO Payout Upon Separation:

When leaving the company, accrued and unused PTO will be paid out to those team members who provide and work at least a two-weeks' notice. The requirement to "work" a two-weeks' notice means that you cannot take PTO during this period, and you must work all scheduled shifts.

Team members who quit with no notice, do not work at least a two-weeks' notice, or who are terminated for any other reason will forfeit any accrued and unused PTO benefits when leaving the Company.

Blackout Periods

The Company, or your specific hotel property, may designate certain periods as a "blackout period", meaning that no team member can take PTO during this period.

Because blackout periods may be announced without lengthy notice, it is important that you not financially commit to travel or other activities during your PTO until your PTO request has been approved in writing by your supervisor.

Holiday Pay

The Company designated seven (7) days each year as holidays:

  • New Year's Day - January 1st
  • Martin Luther King, Jr. Day - Third Monday in January
  • Memorial Day - Last Monday in May
  • Independence Day - July 4th
  • Labor Day - First Monday in September
  • Thanksgiving Day - Fourth Thursday in November
  • Christmas Day - December 25th

Eligibility:

  • Full-time team members are eligible for paid holiday time after 90 days of employment
  • Part-time and on-call/temporary team members are not eligible for holiday pay
  • Team members on a leave of absence during a company designated holiday are not eligible for holiday pay
  • Team members must work their scheduled shifts prior to and after the holiday to be eligible for holiday pay

Pay Procedures:

  • Full-time team members will have 8 hours of holiday pay automatically assigned to their timecard on the day of the holiday
  • Salary team members who work on a Company holiday will be allowed to take another day off during the same pay period with written approval
  • For Monday-Friday schedules: if the holiday falls on Saturday, the previous Friday is the holiday; if it falls on Sunday, the following Monday is the holiday
  • Holiday pay will be paid at the team member's home department/current rate of pay

Time Off to Vote

The Company believes that every team member should have the opportunity to vote in any federal, state, or local election. Team members may be excused for up to three hours to vote in any election for which you are registered to vote.

Team members who need to arrive for work late or leave work early to have a three-hour window in which to vote must notify their supervisor no later than noon on the day before the election.

Time off to vote is unpaid for hourly team members. Salary team members will not have their wages reduced as a result of leaving work to vote.
