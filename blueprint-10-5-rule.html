The 10 & 5 Rule

Guest acknowledgment standards that define "The Zone"

Understanding The Zone

The "10 and 5 Rule" defines The Zone. It is based on two imaginary circles that surround you at all times while on property. Picture yourself standing in the middle of one circle that is 5 feet from you and another placed 10 feet from you.

The 10-Foot Circle

THE MOMENT A GUEST ENTERS THE 10-FOOT CIRCLE YOU SHOULD STOP YOUR CURRENT WORK ACTIVITY AND...

Smile and Make Eye Contact

Key Actions at 10 Feet:

  • Know the POWER of your smile - Your smile is one of the most powerful tools you have
  • Use your smile as a sign to show you are there to help - Let guests know you're available and ready to assist
  • Stop casual conversation in guest presence - Give your full attention to the guest

The 5-Foot Circle

WHEN A GUEST ENTERS THE 5-FOOT CIRCLE AROUND US WE...

Smile and Speak... First and Last

Key Actions at 5 Feet:

  • Greet every guest (and each other) genuinely with a happy "Good Morning" or "Good Afternoon"
  • Greet the guest before they greet you and offer assistance before you are asked
  • Greet guests immediately with your undivided attention
  • Make the first 30 seconds count - First impressions matter
  • Make others feel important - Use the guest's name whenever possible, ask questions about their experience
  • Escort the guest whenever possible - Don't just point; take them where they need to go
  • Make a personal (professional) connection - Be authentic and genuine

Remember

BE YOURSELF - USE YOUR PERSONALITY

The 10 & 5 Rule isn't about being robotic or scripted. It's about being authentically welcoming and ensuring every guest feels acknowledged, valued, and cared for from the moment they enter your zone. Your personality is what makes these interactions memorable.
