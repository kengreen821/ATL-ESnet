Training Topic #36

Premium Rooms

Identifying Arriving Premium Room Types

  1. Identify arriving Premium Room types
    • Follow the arrivals list and RSVP each premium room type as follows:
    • NKSPC - Premium King Bed Handicap Accessible w/ Roll-Out-Sofa and Accessible Full Kitchen (Suite 805)
    • NKSPC-FJLK (Suite 805)
    • NKSPG - Premium Corner Double Bed Suite w/ Pull-Out Sofa (Suite 532)
    • NK, NDS - B&T, 6-KAT1
    • NKSP - Premium Red Suite w/ Pull-Out Sofa (Suite 532)
    • NKSPK - Premium Corner Park View Double Bed Suite w/ Pull-Out Sofa (Suite 6321)
    • NKSPK - $102.82)
    • NKSPK - Premium Park View King Bed Suite w/ Pull-Out Sofa (Suite 6321)
  2. For each, add "Premium Room" to the Guest Request.

    Check Notations

    • If a room does not have any notations on the reservation, check the record history to see if the room type was changed.
    • If it was changed from a standard to a premium by a front desk team member, state "Not a Premium Room."
    • If it was not changed, select "Not a Premium Room."
  3. Double Check Notations on Occupied Premium Rooms for the snacks.

Key Point

Not all guests assigned to premium rooms have paid for the snacks. Sometimes guests are placed in a premium room because they requested it. To avoid confusion, be specific floor will say because no other rooms were available. To avoid confusion in the guest requests when charging the snacks, be sure to select "Not a Premium Room" in the guest requests when charging snacks get replenished every day, except for the day of room type.

  1. Remember, guests get snacks replenished every day, except for the day of checkout.
  2. Place a call in Quore for a premium room to inform housekeeping about a premium room arrival or a premium room replenish.
