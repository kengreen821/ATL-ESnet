Standards of Conduct

Workplace behavior expectations and professional conduct policies

Overview

To assure safety and security and provide the best possible work environment, we expect team members to follow basic, common-sense rules of conduct that will protect everyone's interests and safety.

The Company strives to address performance issues in a manner that will encourage team members to correct behavior immediately and prevent future issues from occurring. For this reason, the Company uses progressive discipline whenever possible.

Progressive Discipline: Generally, the first offense will result in a documented verbal counseling; the second offense will warrant written disciplinary action; and the third offense may result in termination of employment. Depending on the severity of the violation, the team member may enter the disciplinary process at any level as determined by management.

Attendance and Punctuality

Team members are expected to be regular in attendance and to be punctual. Any tardiness or absence causes problems for fellow team members and supervisors.

Team Members are expected to report to work in the assigned work area, in uniform as required, logged in, and at the time they are scheduled. There is no "grace period" from clock-in time to reporting to the work area.

Calling In Absent:

If team members are unable to report for work on any particular day, they must call their supervisor as soon as practical or at least 2 hours before the time the team member is scheduled to begin working for that day.

Important: Leaving a voicemail message with the hotel front desk is not an acceptable method of communicating an absence. Team members must call in on every day they are scheduled to work but will not report to work.

No Call No Show:

If a team member fails to report for work without any notification to their supervisor, and their absence continues for a period of one day, the Company can consider that the team member has abandoned and voluntarily terminated their employment.

Personal Appearance

The image the Company projects to the public is reflected in the appearance of our team members. Team members shall be well-groomed and dressed appropriately for their specific duties.

Professional Appearance Guidelines:

  • Clothing should not constitute a safety hazard
  • Jewelry may be restricted for safety reasons, based on the position
  • All team members should practice common sense rules of cleanliness and comfort
  • When jeans are appropriate for the position, the jeans must be in good condition
  • Tank tops, t-shirts, jogging suits, tennis shoes, flip-flops, slippers, sandals, garments that are unnecessarily revealing, sweatpants and other similar apparel are not permitted
  • Personal appearance should include cleanliness. Facial hair should be clean-shaven or trimmed

Tattoos and Body Piercings:

Tattoos and body piercings are prohibited if they jeopardize personal safety, compromise productivity or performance, are offensive to co-workers or customers, or go against corporate or societal norms.

Prohibited Conduct

The following activities are examples of violations that may warrant progressive discipline. This list is not inclusive of all possible situations:

  • Excessive absenteeism, leaving early, and/or tardiness
  • Violation of established policy, procedure, or safety standard
  • Reporting to work under the influence of drugs or alcohol
  • Possessing, distributing, selling, or using alcohol or illegal drugs in the workplace
  • Fighting or encouraging physical fighting during working hours
  • Harassment or discrimination against team members, guests, or vendors
  • Unauthorized attendance at guest functions or in guestrooms
  • Falsification of any hotel record or document
  • Sharing passwords or not following proper key control procedures
  • Insubordination - refusing to obey instructions from a supervisor
  • Theft or damage of company property or property of team members/guests
  • Job abandonment - walking off the job or leaving assigned work area without permission
  • Vulgarity or arguments with guests, co-workers, or vendors
  • Mishandling confidential information
  • Working overtime without authorization
Note: Depending on the severity of the violation, the team member may enter the disciplinary process at any level (verbal, written, suspension or termination) as determined by management. A severe violation may result in immediate termination.

Social Media Policy

Team members are prohibited from:

  • Using social media while on working time unless it is being used for Company business and with authorization
  • Posting photographs of vendors, suppliers, or customers on social media without their express permission
  • Disclosing or publishing promotional content about the Company without authorization
  • Using employer-owned equipment for personal blogging or social networking activities

The Company has the right to monitor the use of its information technology and any publicly accessible social media. Team members should expect that any information created or discussed on publicly accessible online social media may be accessed by the Company at any time without prior notice.
